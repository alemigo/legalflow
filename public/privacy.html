eNDA Technologies Privacy Policy

Last Updated: December 5, 2025

This Privacy Policy explains how ENDA Technologies, LLC (“eNDA,” “we,” “our,” “us”) collects, uses, discloses, and protects personal information when individuals and organizations access or use eNDA’s mobile / desktop websites, applications, platforms, artificial intelligence tools, electronic signature tools, document-generation tools, and related products and services (collectively, the “Services”).

By accessing or using the Services, you agree to the terms of this Privacy Policy.

eNDA may act as either a data controller or a data processor depending on the context of processing, as explained below.

1. Scope and Application

This Privacy Policy applies to personal information we process in connection with:

  • The eNDA platform, including document creation, editing, negotiation, signature, workflow automation, AI-based features, communications and storage.
  • Our websites, applications, marketing pages, and customer-support portals.
  • Communications with eNDA representatives, including sales, onboarding, and support teams.
  • Data used to provide analytics, improve the Services, and train certain AI and machine-learning models.

This policy does not apply to third-party services you integrate with eNDA, or to User Content (defined below) where your organization acts as the controller and eNDA acts solely as the processor.

2. Controller and Processor Roles

eNDA as Processor

For any information that customers or their users upload, create, edit, store, or manage using the Services (“User Content”), eNDA processes that information only on behalf of the customer and according to the customer’s instructions. This includes:

  • Documents and templates
  • Data fields, clauses, toggles, and redlines
  • Uploaded files
  • Signature workflows, audit trails, and timestamps
  • Communications within a workspace
  • Recipient information and workflow metadata

For User Content, the customer (or the customer’s administrator) is the data controller and is responsible for handling privacy rights requests related to such data.

eNDA as Controller

eNDA acts as the data controller for information we collect for our own business and operational purposes. This includes:

  • Account registration and authentication
  • Billing and payment data
  • Usage analytics and product performance data
  • Marketing, sales, and business development
  • Security, fraud detection, and compliance
  • Optional identity verification features
  • AI model training using de-identified or aggregated data (see Section 7)

3. Information We Collect

eNDA collects the following categories of personal information. The specific information collected depends on your interactions with the Services.

A. Identifiers and Contact Information

  • Name and email address
  • Phone number
  • Organization name, job title, and role
  • Account login credentials
  • IP address and device identifiers

B. Account, Billing, and Payment Information

  • Subscription plan, billing address, invoice history
  • Payment card information collected and processed by Stripe or another payment processor
  • eNDA does not store full payment card numbers

C. User Content (Processor Role)

  • Document contents
  • Data entered into fields, clauses, templates, and workflows
  • Uploaded attachments
  • Comments, negotiation notes, and redlines
  • Names, emails, and workflow metadata for signers and recipients
  • Document-view events, signature events, authentication steps, and audit-trail elements

D. Usage, Event, and Technical Data

  • Log files and diagnostics
  • Device type, browser type, operating system
  • IP address and approximate geolocation
  • Clickstream data, feature usage patterns, and interaction metrics
  • Error reports and performance analytics
  • Document event history (viewed, opened, edited, signed, etc.)

E. Optional Identity Verification or Biometric Information

If your organization enables identity verification via Stripe Identity or another provider, eNDA may receive confirmation of verification results. Information processed by the vendor may include:

  • Facial images or biometric templates
  • Images of government-issued identification
  • Verification attributes (pass/fail, verified name, verified email)

eNDA does not store raw biometric data. Such data is stored by the identity-verification provider under its own privacy terms.

F. Marketing, Lead Generation, and Engagement Data

  • Web activity on eNDA websites
  • Email-open data, click tracking, and engagement metrics
  • Form submissions, trial sign-ups, and demo requests
  • Preferences for communication and product categories
  • Information used for cross-sell, upsell, or personalized recommendations

G. Cookies, Tracking Technologies, and Behavioral Advertising

We use cookies, web beacons, pixels, tags, and similar technologies to support:

  • Site functionality
  • Authentication
  • Analytics and product optimization
  • A/B testing
  • Marketing, advertising, and personalization
  • Cross-context behavioral advertising where permitted by applicable law

Cookie preferences may be managed through eNDA’s cookie settings.

eNDA uses personal information for the following business purposes.

A. Providing the Services

  • Creating, editing, and managing documents
  • Maintaining signature workflows, audit trails, and verification logs
  • Operating clause toggles, template libraries, and document automation

B. Improving the Services

  • Feature optimization and performance analysis

  • Enhancing usability, workflows, and user experience

C. AI and Machine Learning

D. Analytics and Research

  • Conducting benchmarking and platform-wide analysis

E. Marketing and Cross-Selling

  • Presenting relevant partner services or future eNDA products
  • Conducting lead-nurturing campaigns
  • Measuring marketing effectiveness

You may opt out of marketing communications at any time.

F. Security and Compliance

  • Detecting unauthorized access and unusual activity
  • Preventing fraud and abuse
  • Verifying identity
  • Complying with subpoenas, legal requests, and regulatory obligations
  • Enforcing our terms of service

7. AI and Machine Learning Practices

This section applies to all eNDA AI features, including document drafting, clause recommendations, analysis tools, and risk scoring.

Default Practices

eNDA uses de-identified or aggregated data derived from User Content to build, train, and maintain AI models that power certain Services. De-identified data is processed in a way that cannot reasonably be linked back to an identifiable individual or organization.

Customer Controls

Customers may opt out of the use of de-identified data from their accounts for AI improvement purposes.

Identifiable User Content

eNDA does not use identifiable User Content to train general-purpose AI models unless expressly agreed to in writing or enabled through administrative settings.
